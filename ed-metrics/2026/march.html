Operational Insights
🚑 Insight 1 · Volume Surge
March Was the Highest-Volume Month on Record — Weekday Average Surpassed 155 Patients/Day
With 4,620 total visits and a weekday average of 155.8 patients/day, March exceeded April's volume by 309 visits (+7.2%). Monday was the peak day at 170.6 average — with individual Mondays reaching 181 patients. This level of weekday volume substantially exceeds the 142-patient high-volume threshold used for staffing triggers, with 16 of 22 weekdays qualifying as high-volume days. March likely required supplemental staffing on most weekdays, reinforcing the value of the noon trigger protocol.
⚠️ Insight 2 · Timeliness Decline
ESI 2 <25-Minute Rate Dropped to 83% — Volume-Driven Degradation Needs Monitoring
ESI 2 timeliness fell from 88.3% in April to 83.0% in March, approaching the 80% alert threshold. Importantly, no individual hour exceeded the 25-minute target (peak was 20.3 min at 19:00) — meaning the decline is driven by overall throughput pressure rather than a specific time-of-day failure. With a weekday volume of 155.8 patients/day in March, this performance is still commendable, but the trend requires active monitoring as volume continues to grow.
🛏️ Insight 3 · Boarding Worsening
Admit LOS Climbed to 386 Minutes — Boarding Burden Increased Month-Over-Month
Median admit LOS worsened from 371 min (April) to 386 min (March), now reaching 6 hours 26 minutes. With 866 admissions in March (18.7% of visits), admitted patients are occupying ED beds for an average of 2 hr 50 min longer than discharged patients. This is an accelerating trend. The 15-minute month-over-month increase, if sustained, projects to boarding times exceeding 7 hours by summer. Inpatient throughput escalation and bed assignment SLA enforcement should be treated as urgent priorities.
⚠️ Insight 4 · D2D Outliers
Door-to-Doc Outliers >120 Minutes Spiked to 216 — 2.5× April's Rate
While the median D2D was only 3 minutes higher than April (21 vs 18 min), extreme outliers >120 minutes jumped dramatically to 216 encounters versus April's 87. This 2.5× increase is disproportionate to the volume increase and suggests documentation or registration workflow issues that inflate apparent D2D at high-volume times. A spot audit of these 216 records — particularly checking whether arrival time, registration time, and provider assessment time are being captured correctly — is warranted before accepting these as true clinical delays.
🔴 Insight 5 · LOS Goal Breach
First Provider to Exceed 300-Minute LOS Goal: BATIZY L at 304.1 Minutes
BATIZY L is the first provider to exceed the 300-minute discharge LOS goal (304.1 min, n=22), compared to the department best of 160.0 min (BUNKER C). This is a new finding not present in April data and warrants a direct, supportive coaching conversation. The 144-minute spread between the best and highest-LOS providers is wider than April's 113-minute range, suggesting throughput variance is growing as overall volume increases.
✅ Insight 6 · Productivity Win
Highest-Volume Month Also Produced Best Productivity — Both Pt/Hr and aPPH Above Goal
March's elevated volume translated into strong productivity: Pt/Hr = 2.02 (vs goal 1.85, and vs April's 1.89) and aPPH = 2.58 (vs goal 2.45, and vs April's 2.41 which was below goal). Both metrics are now green for the first time. This confirms the team is handling volume efficiently — the key challenge is managing the downstream effects of that volume on timeliness and boarding, not productivity itself. Confirm March hours to finalize these figures.

Recommended Actions
1
Operationalize the Noon Trigger — Now Validated Across 2 Months: The ≥54–55 patients by noon threshold has held up in both March (PPV 93%) and April (PPV 91%). Present to TeamHealth scheduling as a standing escalation protocol. Charge RN checks cumulative arrivals at noon daily; if threshold met, attending is notified to initiate supplemental coverage review.
2
Escalate Boarding to Hospital Leadership — Trend is Accelerating: Admit LOS rose 15 minutes month-over-month (371→386 min) during the highest-volume month on record. Present 2-month boarding trend with an urgent request for a bed assignment SLA (<60 min from admit order) and a formal boarding escalation protocol for patients boarding >4 hours.
3
Audit the 216 D2D >120-Min Outliers: Pull a sample of these records and verify the registration, triage, and provider assessment timestamps are being captured accurately. If documentation gaps are found, a registration/triage workflow correction will improve reported metrics without requiring clinical changes.
4
Initiate Coaching for BATIZY L: First provider to breach the 300-min goal. Schedule a brief, constructive conversation framing their LOS data in the context of peer benchmarks and the department goal. Focus on early disposition planning for ESI 3/4/5 patients and fast-track pathway adherence.
5
Monitor ESI 2 Timeliness Closely — Do Not Let It Drop Below 80%: At 83%, the department is 3 points above the alert threshold during a record-volume month. This is resilient but not comfortable. Validate that provider-in-triage or fast-track coverage is maintained on all high-volume weekdays, particularly Mondays.
