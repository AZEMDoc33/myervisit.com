Director View — April 2026
Operational Insights · Recommended Actions · Data Quality Notes · Not visible to clinical staff
💡
Operational Insights
⚠️ Insight 1 · Throughput
Afternoon Staffing Gap Driving 14:00 ESI 2 Door-to-Doc Spike
ESI 2 door-to-doc peaks at 35.8 minutes at 14:00 — more than 3× the median of 11 minutes and above the 25-minute standard. Arrivals remain elevated through 17:00–18:00. This pattern suggests a staffing transition gap in the early afternoon. Shift overlap and triage resource coverage from 13:00–16:00 should be reviewed.
🛏️ Insight 2 · Boarding
6h 11m Admit LOS Indicates Significant Boarding Burden
Median admit LOS of 371 minutes vs discharge LOS of 220 minutes means admitted patients occupy ED beds ~2.5 additional hours. With 822 admissions per month (19.1%), this creates substantial throughput pressure. A timed bed assignment SLA with escalation beyond 4 hours of boarding is warranted.
👨‍⚕️ Insight 3 · Provider Performance
113-Minute Range in ESI 3/4/5 LOS Across Provider Panel — All Under 300-Min Goal
Discharge LOS ranges from 155.7 min (RIGBY D) to 268.7 min (HAMBLIN L) — a 113-minute spread. All 25 providers are under the 300-minute goal, which is a genuine department win. However, narrowing the gap between the upper and lower tertiles remains a throughput opportunity worth addressing through peer benchmarking and early disposition protocols.
✅ Insight 4 · Strengths
ESI 2 Timeliness and LWOT Rate Are Genuine Department Wins
88.3% of ESI 2 patients seen within 25 minutes (median 11 min), and LWOT rate of 1.4% is commendable for a 144-patient/day department. These should be highlighted in leadership reporting and shared with the team as evidence that front-end workflows are performing well.
🎯
Recommended Actions
1
Deploy Noon Staffing Trigger Protocol: When noon cumulative arrivals hit ≥55, charge RN notifies attending to initiate supplemental coverage discussion. 91% PPV makes this operationally defensible.
2
Address 13:00–16:00 Staffing Overlap Gap: ESI 2 D2D spikes to 35.8 min at 14:00. Ensure provider-in-triage or fast-track coverage extends through 16:00. Review afternoon shift start times.
3
Boarding Reduction Project with Hospital Operations: Present 371-min median admit LOS with a proposal for a 60-minute bed assignment SLA post-admit order, plus an escalation pathway for patients boarding >4 hours.
4
Peer Benchmarking for Upper-Tertile LOS Providers: Share deidentified LOS comparison data. Frame as throughput coaching. Target: narrow top-to-bottom gap from 113 min toward 60 min over 2 reporting cycles.
5
Sustain LWOT and ESI 2 Performance: Set alert thresholds: LWOT >2.5% or ESI 2 <25-min rate below 80% triggers a rapid process review. Celebrate current performance with the team.

Data Quality Notes
✅
LOS >24 hours: 20 records excluded from all LOS metrics. These had checkout timestamps representing inpatient hospital discharge rather than ED departure. The most extreme outlier showed a checkout year of 2100 (clear registration error). All 20 were "Admit to Inpatient" or "Acute Care Facility (Banner)" dispositions — appropriate to exclude for ED throughput metrics.
✅
78 encounters missing primary physician — largely explained by LWOT. LWOT and eloped patients (n=60) leave before provider assignment, accounting for the majority of this gap. True unexplained missing: ~18 records (<0.4% of non-LWOT encounters). No action required.
⚠️
24 encounters missing acuity (ESI level) — 0.6% of total. Excluded from acuity-stratified metrics. Recommend triage documentation reminder.
⚠️
87 door-to-doc values exceed 120 minutes. Likely registration/documentation delays. Included in analysis but flagged. Maximum: 241 minutes. Spot-check recommended.
✅
No negative LOS values. Disposition categories clean and consistent.
