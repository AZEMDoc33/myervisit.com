Operational Insights
✅ Insight 1 · January Context
January Was the Strongest Month Operationally — Best LOS, Best D2D, and Lowest LWOT of the Quarter
January delivered the best performance on almost every metric: median DC LOS 198 min, overall D2D 15 min, ESI 2 D2D all hours under target, LWOT rate 0.8% (35 patients), and only 62 D2D outliers over 120 min. This likely reflects lower overall volume (140.2/day vs Feb's 154.2) but also suggests January's staffing mix and operational rhythms were genuinely more efficient. Understanding why January performed this well is as valuable as understanding why March was harder — identify the operational levers and replicate them.
📊 Insight 2 · Q1 LOS Trend
Discharge LOS Has Risen Every Month From January to April — 22-Minute Q1 Increase Warrants Attention
Median discharge LOS rose from 198 min (Jan) → 207 (Feb) → 216 (Mar) → 220 (Apr) — a 22-minute drift upward across the quarter. This is not explained by volume alone (April's volume was lower than February's, yet LOS was higher). The trend likely reflects growing boarding pressure as admitted patients block discharge beds, worsening throughput downstream. If this trajectory continues, the department will approach 240-minute discharge LOS by mid-summer, which will create significant Press Ganey exposure.
⚠️ Insight 3 · New Providers
Two New Providers in January Data — JONES M and STOWERS R — with Limited but High-LOS Pattern
JONES M (n=19, LOS 174.4 min) and STOWERS R (n=29, LOS 184.8 min) appear in January data without prior month representation. Their LOS values are in the middle tertile and not concerning individually, but given their limited sample sizes, monitoring over the next 2–3 months before drawing conclusions is appropriate. MILLER E (n=15, LOS 253.3 min) also appears — in the upper tertile from first observation, which warrants early tracking.
✅ Insight 4 · Productivity
January aPPH = 2.45 — First Month to Exactly Hit the Goal in Q1
January's adjusted PPH of 2.45 exactly meets the goal, with Pt/Hr of 1.91 comfortably above 1.85. Combined with the favorable LOS and D2D data, January demonstrates that the department can hit productivity targets even with a full patient panel. The slight volume dip in January (vs Feb–Mar) helped, but the efficiency per visit was also better. Confirm January hours to finalize these figures.
🛏️ Insight 5 · Boarding Origin
January Admit LOS of 336 Min Was Lowest of Q1 — Confirms Boarding Worsened as Volume Rose
The Q1 boarding trend is clear: Jan 336 → Feb 368 → Mar 386 → Apr 371 min. January's admit LOS was 50 minutes lower than the Q1 peak in March. This correlation with volume strongly suggests that inpatient capacity is the limiting variable — when the ED sends more patients upstairs, the backup at the point of admission grows. A formal capacity analysis with hospital operations is now supported by 4 months of consistent data.

Recommended Actions
1
Formalize the ≥50-Noon Staffing Trigger — 4 Months of Evidence: PPV 83–100% across all 4 months. Draft a 1-page protocol for TeamHealth with the validation table. The case is now unambiguous. Present at next leadership meeting with a request to build it into the daily charge nurse checklist.
2
Escalate the Q1 Boarding Trend to Hospital Leadership Immediately: 4 months of data showing admit LOS rising from 336 to 386 min in correlation with volume. Prepare a 2-slide presentation: (1) the month-over-month boarding trend with volume overlay, (2) a formal proposal for a bed assignment SLA and escalation protocol for >4-hour boarders. Request a formal meeting with CNO or COO.
3
Investigate What Made January Operationally Better: Pull shift schedules, fast-track utilization rates, and provider overlap patterns for January vs March. Was there more midlevel coverage? Different provider-in-triage hours? Fewer admits backing up the department? Whatever drove the 22-minute LOS advantage and 0.8% LWOT should be identified and protected.
4
Monitor MILLER E, JONES M, STOWERS R Over Next 2 Months: New providers with small samples — don't act yet, but flag for review in February and March reports. MILLER E's 253.3 min on first observation warrants informal check-in at the 3-month mark.
5
Present Q1 2026 Summary Report to Full Provider Team: With 4 months now published, produce a 1-page Q1 summary (volume trend, LOS trend, productivity, LWOT, trigger validation) for distribution to all providers. Frame January's strong performance as the benchmark the team should aspire to replicate.
